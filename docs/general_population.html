What we know with HIGH CONFIDENCE (from CMC research):
When caregivers of medically complex children receive multicomponent interventions, their loneliness decreases by 0.80 points (Hoang et al., JAMA Network Open, 2022), which reduces their child's school absences by ~0.39 days (Family CIRCLE data analysis). This is calculated by taking the loneliness frequency, statistically-significant beta coefficient from Family Circle analysis (0.482) and multiplying it by the intervention effect (0.8) derived from the JAMA study.

What we're ESTIMATING (general population):
General population parents also experience high loneliness (66% report isolation - Ohio State University Wexner Medical Center, 2024). The same interventions might reduce absences for all children, but the effect could be weaker because:

  • Healthy children's absences may be less driven by caregiver stress
  • The loneliness ‚Üí absence relationship may be CMC-specific
  • We have no direct data, only reasonable assumptions

Bottom line: Use these estimates to understand potential scale, not as confirmed findings. Wide uncertainty ranges reflect our limited knowledge.

  • Simulation approach: We simulate 10,000 children (sample) and extrapolate to full population. The per-child effect is identical, so this is statistically equivalent but much faster.
